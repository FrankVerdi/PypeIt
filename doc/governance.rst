PypeIt Governance Structure
===========================

PypeIt follows a structured do-ocracy governance model, where valuable and
sustained contributions to the project lead to more influence over its
development direction via the roles and committees defined below.

.. note::

    We are currently in a transition to a new model that evolves the current
    structure into one that is intended to be more community focused, but
    remains primarily a do-ocracy.  We expect the new governance model will be
    instituted by the end of 2025.  The following is **a working draft** of this
    governance structure.

Coordination Committee
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The role of the Coordination Committee is to consolidate the needs of the PypeIt
community into a set of high-level development milestones.  I

helps direct the overall development effort toward a
set of goals informed by the needs of the PypeIt community.

  Facilitates the high-level development of work packages and
  development milestones.

  It has four members:

- **Project Scientist**: Guides the overall scientific direction of the
  project.

- **Developer Committee Chair**: Presents and advocates for priorities of the
  developer community.

- **End-User Committee Chair**: Presents and advocates for priorities of the
  end-user community.

- **Advisory Council Representative**: Presents and advocates for priorities of
  the broader astronomical community, both those that do and do not currently
  use PypeIt for spectroscopic data reduction.

Developer and End-User Committees
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Ultimately, the success of PypeIt will rely on **developers** who are willing
and able to improve and maintain the code and **end-users** that can
successfully use it to extract data for direct scientific analysis from raw
observations.  PypeIt encourages significant overlap between these two groups;
i.e., we encourage end-users to develop the code and developers to use PypeIt
for science projects.  The purpose of having distinct Developer and End-User
Committees, however, are to ensure that the needs specific to each group are
well represented within the Coordination Committee.

Each committee is empowered to setup its own structure.  However, each committee
must elect a chair that will also become part of the Coordination Committee.
The duties of each chair are:

 - Facilitate the collection of information from developers/end-users regarding
   issues with PypeIt, ranging from minor bugs to significant feature
   deficiencies

 - Convene and lead committee meetings to discuss these developer/end-user
   issues

 - Present and advocate for development effort needed to address those needs as
   part of the Coordination Committee

In addition to communicating the Coordination Committee discussions to its
Committee members, the Developer Committee Chair will encourage development
toward the milestones developed by the Coordination Committee.  Similarly, the
End-User Committee Chair will encourage testing efforts for new/improved
functionality that stems from each milestone.
